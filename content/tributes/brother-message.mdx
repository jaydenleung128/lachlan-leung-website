So many stories I could tell. But today, I want to share one moment that, for me, really captures who he was.

When we were in Hong Kong, we went to the World Table Tennis Finals event. Our family secured incredible courtside seats with the help of our friend Wade Townsend. We couldn't get any closer to the action and we were honestly, extremely happy.

But for Locky, he wanted more.

The famous table tennis commentator, Adam Bobrow, had just finished an interview and was packing up his things for the day. Whilst the rest of us were standing back, Locky jumped out of his seat, sprinted around the grandstand, waved his hands in excitement and caught the attention of Adam.

Within minutes, Locky had managed to get Adam to follow him back on Instagram. Knowing that Adam had connections with WTT, Locky asked if there was a chance he could meet his favourite TT player, Shin Yubin.

And somehow, this simple question turned into a private meet and greet, where Locky not only received a signed Shin Yubin shirt but also scored a big cuddle with her.

Moments like that really capture who Locky was. He was courageous, determined, and quietly confident, never content with just standing back or simply accepting what was in front of him. Locky always wanted to get the most out of the moment.

Watching him taught me something I'll always remember. Don't be afraid to ask for what you want, because sometimes the only thing holding you back is yourself.

Locky, thank you for showing me how to be a little braver, a little more open, and a little less afraid.

Thank you for the memories and laughs. I'm so proud to call you, my brother.

I'm going to miss you a lot. But I'll always carry you with me, in the stories I tell, the memories I hold, and the way I live my life.

Until we meet again, I love you Locky.
